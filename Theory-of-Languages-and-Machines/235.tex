\begin{flushleft}
\textbf{6.7 .1 Chomsky Normal Form}
\end{flushleft}
A CFG is said to be in CNF if all the productions of the grammar are in the following form 


\begin{center}
	
Non-terminal $\rightarrow$ String of exactly two non-terminals

Non-terminal $\rightarrow$ Single terminal
\end{center}

A CFG can be converted into CNF by the following process.

\begin{flushleft}
\textbf{Step I:}

1. Eliminate all the $\epsilon$ -production.

2. Eliminate all the unit production.

3. Eliminate all the useless symbols.
\end{flushleft}

\begin{flushleft}
\textbf{Step II:}

1. \textbf{if} (all the productions are in the form $\mathrm{NT} \rightarrow$ string of exactly two NTs or $\mathrm{NT} \rightarrow$ Single terminal) Declare the CFG is in CNF. And stop.


2. \textbf{else} (follow step III and/or IV and/or V). 

\end{flushleft}

\begin{flushleft}
\textbf{Step III:} (Elimination of terminals on the RHS of length two or more.) Consider any production of the form
$$
\mathrm{NT} \rightarrow \mathrm{T}_{1} \mathrm{~T}_{2} \ldots . \mathrm{T}_{n}
$$
where $\mathrm{n} \geq 2$ (T means terminal and NT means noo-terminal). For a terminal $\mathrm{T}$, introduce a new variable (non-terminal) say $\mathrm{NT}$, and a corresponding production $\mathrm{NT}, \rightarrow \mathrm{T}$, Repeat this for every terminal on the RHS so that every production of the grammar has either a single terminal or two or more variables.
\end{flushleft}

\begin{flushleft}
\textbf{Step Iv: }(Restriction of the number of variables on the RHS to two.) Consider any production of the form
$$
\mathrm{NT} \rightarrow \mathrm{NT}_{\mathrm{1}} \mathrm{NT}_{2} \ldots \mathrm{NT}_{n}
$$
where $\mathrm{n} \geq 3$.

The production $\mathrm{NT} \rightarrow \mathrm{NT}_{1} \mathrm{NT}_{2} \ldots \mathrm{NT}_{n}$ is replaced by new productions
\begin{center}
$\mathrm{NT} \rightarrow \mathrm{NT}_{1} \mathrm{~A}_{1}$

$\mathrm{~A}_{1} \rightarrow \mathrm{NT}_{2} \mathrm{~A}_{2}$

$\mathrm{~A}_{2} \rightarrow \mathrm{NT}_{3} \mathrm{~A}_{3}$

.............................

.............................

$\mathrm{A}_{\mathrm{a}-2} \rightarrow \mathrm{NT}_{\mathrm{n}-1} \mathrm{NT}_{\mathrm{n}}$
\end{center}
	
Here the $\mathrm{~A}_{1}$ 's are new variables.

\end{flushleft}
