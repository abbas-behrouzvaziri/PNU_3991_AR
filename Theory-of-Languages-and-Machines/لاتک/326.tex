\textbf{Step v :} (Conversion of the string containing terminals and non-terminals to the string of non-terminal. on the RHS) Consider any production of the form
$$
\mathrm{NT} \rightarrow \mathrm{T}_{1} \mathrm{~T}_{2} \ldots . \mathrm{T}_{n} \mathrm{NT}_{1} \ldots . \mathrm{NT}_{n^{,}}
$$
where $n \geq 1$ 
For the part $\mathrm{T}_{1} \mathrm{~T}_{2} \ldots \mathrm{T}_{\mathrm{n},}$ follow Step III

Check the resultant string by Step II. If not in CNF, follow Step IV. 

By this process, the generated new grammar is in CNF.

\begin{flushleft}
\includegraphics[scale=0.5]{6.37}  Comvert the following grammar into CNF.
\end{flushleft}
$$
\begin{array}{l}
	S \rightarrow b A / a B \\
	A \rightarrow b A A / a S / a \\
	B \rightarrow a B B / D S / a
\end{array}
$$
\textbf{Selation:} The productions $S \rightarrow b A, S \rightarrow a B, A \rightarrow b A A, A \rightarrow a S, B \rightarrow a B B, B \rightarrow b S$ are not in CNF.
So, we have to coavert these into CNF. Let us replace terminal 'a' by a non-temminal $\mathrm{c}_{a}$ and terminal 'b' by a noa-teminal $C_{b}$. Hence, two new productions will be added to the granimar
$$
C_{a} \rightarrow a \text { and } C_{b} \rightarrow b
$$
By replacing a and b by new non-terminals and including the two productions, the modified grammar will be

\begin{center}
	

$\mathrm{S} \rightarrow \mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{b}} \mathrm{A} / \mathrm{C}_{a}
\mathrm{B}$

$\mathrm{A} \rightarrow \mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{b}} \mathrm{AA} /
 \mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{a}}  \mathrm{S/a}$
 
$\mathrm{B} \rightarrow \mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{a}} \mathrm{BB} / \mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{b}} {\mathrm{S}} / \mathrm{a}$

$\mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{a}} \rightarrow \mathrm{a}$

$\mathrm{C}_{b} \rightarrow \mathrm{b}$

\end{center}

In the modified grammar, all the productions are not in CNF. The productions $A \rightarrow {C}_{b}$ AA and $B \rightarrow$ ${C}_{a}$ BB are not in CNF, becanse they contain more than two noa-terminals at the RHS. Let us replace AA by a new non-terminal $D$ and BB by another new non-terminal E. Hence, two new productions will be added to the grammar $\mathrm{D} \rightarrow \mathrm{AA}$ and $\mathrm{E} \rightarrow \mathrm{BB}$. So, the new modified grammar will be
 \begin{center}
$\mathrm{S} \rightarrow \mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{b}} \mathrm{A} / \mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{a}} \mathrm{B}$

$\mathrm{A} \rightarrow \mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{b}} \mathrm{D} / \mathrm{C}_{a}{\mathrm{S} / \mathrm{a}}$

$\mathrm{B} \rightarrow \mathrm{C}_{b} \mathrm{E} / \mathrm{C}_{a} \mathrm{~S} / \mathrm{a}$

$\mathrm{D} \rightarrow \mathrm{AA}$

$\mathrm{E} \rightarrow \mathrm{BB}$

$\mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{a}} \rightarrow \mathrm{a}$

$\mathrm{C}_{\mathrm{b}} \rightarrow \mathrm{b}$ 
\end{center}
